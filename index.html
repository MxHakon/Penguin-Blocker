Privacy Policy

Penguin Blocker - Transparent & Secure

Last updated: June 15, 2025

Overview

Penguin Blocker ("we", "our", or "us") is a Chrome extension that provides basic content filtering functionality. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our extension.

Information We Collect

Local Storage Data

  • Extension Settings: Your preferences for blocking features, notifications, and domain-specific settings
  • Statistics: Local counters for blocked content (daily and total counts)
  • Disabled Domains: List of websites where you've disabled blocking
  • Configuration Data: Extension state and user preferences

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect any personal information
  • We do not track your browsing history
  • We do not collect data about specific websites you visit
  • We do not send any data to external servers
  • We do not use analytics or tracking services
  • We do not collect cookies or device identifiers

How We Use Information

All data collected by Penguin Blocker is stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage API and is used solely to:

  • Remember your extension settings and preferences
  • Display blocking statistics within the extension
  • Maintain your domain-specific blocking preferences
  • Provide the core functionality of the extension

Data Storage and Security

  • All data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's secure storage system
  • No data is transmitted to external servers
  • Data is only accessible by the Penguin Blocker extension
  • You can clear all stored data at any time through the extension's settings

Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or disclose any information to third parties. Since all data is stored locally on your device, there is no external data sharing.

Your Rights and Controls

You have complete control over your data:

  • View Data: Access all stored data through the extension interface
  • Delete Data: Clear all data using the "Clear All Data" button in settings
  • Export Data: Export your disabled domains list for backup purposes
  • Uninstall: Removing the extension deletes all associated data

Permissions Explanation

Penguin Blocker requires the following Chrome permissions:

  • declarativeNetRequest: To block ads and trackers using Chrome's native blocking API
  • storage: To save your settings and preferences locally
  • activeTab: To determine the current website domain for site-specific controls
  • tabs: To manage blocking rules per website
  • host_permissions: To apply blocking rules across all websites

Children's Privacy

Our extension does not knowingly collect information from children under 13. The extension is designed to be safe for users of all ages.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Legal Compliance

This extension complies with:

  • Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
  • Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)
